Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of counselling do you offer?

I work integratively, which means I draw on a range of therapeutic approaches. But my core is person-centred — so our work is led by you, not a manual. It’s collaborative, respectful, and paced around what you need.

What’s the difference between counselling and coaching?

Good question — they can overlap, but generally counselling focuses on healing, reflection and emotional insight, while coaching is more future-oriented, helping you make decisions or move through transitions. If you’re unsure, I’m happy to talk it through.

How much do sessions cost?

Sessions are £60 and last 50 minutes. Some lower-cost spaces are available, especially for those on reduced income — just ask if that would help.

Do you offer online sessions?

Yes — I work both in person and online. Some people like to mix and match depending on what’s happening in their week.

What if I’m not sure this is right for me?

You don’t have to decide everything at once. An initial session can be a gentle way to see how it feels. There’s no pressure to continue if it doesn’t feel right.

What happens in the first session?

We’ll talk about what’s bringing you here and what you’d like from the process. It’s also a chance for you to get a sense of whether you’d like to work with me — that matters just as much.

Is everything confidential?

Yes — confidentiality is central to the work. There are a few exceptions (e.g. if there’s serious risk to you or someone else), which I’ll explain clearly before we start.

Can I stop at any time?

Absolutely. You’re not tied in, and you can pause or end whenever feels right. We can also talk about what a thoughtful ending might look like if that’s helpful.

I’m worried about opening up too much. Is that okay?

It’s completely okay. We go at your pace, and there’s no expectation to share more than feels manageable. Trust takes time, and I’ll meet you with patience and care.

Do you work with men specifically?

Yes — I have a particular interest in men’s mental health and the things that often go unsaid. That said, I work with adults of all genders, backgrounds, and experiences.

How often are sessions?

Most clients come weekly, but we can also explore fortnightly sessions if that’s more sustainable for you. We’ll agree what works best together.

What if I can’t afford it every week?

Let’s talk about it. I want therapy to be accessible where possible, and I’m open to discussing flexible options if you're committed but cost is a barrier.

Where are you based?

I’m based in Manchester and offer in-person sessions in a comfortable, quiet space. Online sessions are available to clients across the UK.

How do I get started?

Just get in touch — you can email, call, text or WhatsApp me. I’ll reply as soon as I can, and we’ll take it from there.
