Counselling with Me In Time

Sometimes, we reach a point where something needs to shift — but we don’t quite know how to begin. Maybe life feels heavy. Maybe it’s been that way for a while. Or maybe something’s happened that’s left you feeling knocked or lost. You’re not alone.

Counselling offers you a space to breathe. A space to speak freely, without judgement. To feel heard — really heard — and begin to make sense of what’s going on. There’s no pressure here. Just time set aside for you, at your own pace, in your own way.

A Calm, Grounded Approach

I work in an integrative way — blending different approaches depending on what feels right for you. But at the heart of it all is a person-centred foundation. That means we begin wherever you are, and we build the work together.

This isn’t about quick fixes or being analysed from a distance. It’s about feeling safe enough to be honest. Held enough to explore what hurts. Respected enough to take your time. My aim is to offer a steady presence, so you can begin to reconnect with yourself — gently, honestly, and without masks.

What We Might Explore

There’s no one reason people come to therapy. Sometimes it’s a clear event. Sometimes it’s a slow build. Often, it’s just a sense that something doesn’t feel right anymore.

You might be living with:

  • Persistent anxiety, low mood, or burnout
  • Grief, loss, or unresolved pain
  • A harsh inner critic or low self-worth
  • Relationship or family challenges
  • Questions of identity, meaning, or direction
  • Feeling stuck — and unsure why

Men often tell me it’s hard to even find the words — especially when they’ve been taught to “just get on with it.” You don’t need to have the language, or the answers, to begin. We can find them together.

Practicalities

Sessions are 50 minutes and usually weekly — though we can discuss what rhythm works for you. I offer both in-person appointments in Manchester and online sessions.

Standard Fee: £60 per session. A limited number of lower-cost places are available — just ask if that would help.

If you're curious or want to talk things through, you're welcome to get in touch. No pressure — just an open invitation.
